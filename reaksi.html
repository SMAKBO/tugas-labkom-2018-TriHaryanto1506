Reaction of Acetic Acid

How Reaction Between Acetic Acid With Other Compound ?

Acetic Acid can react with other compound there is with Anorganic and Organic Compound

Acetic Acid with Anorganic Compound

Acetic Acid is corrosive to metals produce hydrogen gas and acetat metal. The acetate metal may be obtained by the reaction of acetic acid with a base. All acetate salts dissolve completely in water except Chromium (III) Acetate. This is Example of acetic salt formation reaction:

Mg (s) + 2CH3COOH(aq) --> (CH3COO)2Mg(aq)

Acetic Acid With Organic Compound

Acetic acid undergoes carboxylic acid reactions such as producing acetic salts when reacting with alkali. With a strong base like an orhanolithium reagent acetic acid producing LiCH2CO2Li. The most famous organic reaction of acetic acid is the bleaching of ethanol by reduction. anhydrous acetate is formed by condensation of two acetic acid molecules. esters of acetic acid can be obtained by the esterfication reaction of the fischer and also the amide formation.

Can we detect acetic acid ?

Yes we can, acetic acid can be detected by the smell. In addition, the acetic salt will react with the Iron (III) Chloride and show solid red color. and the acetic salt will react with Arsenic Trioxida (AsO3) at high temperatures and produce kakodil oxide which has a characteristic smell.
